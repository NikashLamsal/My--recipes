Croissant

Description

Croissants are a flaky, buttery pastry made by layering dough with butter through a process called laminating. Shaped into a crescent, they are baked until golden and crisp. Croissants can be enjoyed plain or filled with sweet or savory ingredients, making them a versatile and beloved treat.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cou granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1 cup butter (cold)
  • 1 egg

Steps

  1. Make Dough: Mix flour, sugar, salt, yeast, milk, and water to form a dough. Chill for 1 hour.
  2. Prepare Butter: Flatten cold butter into a square sheet and chill.
  3. Laminate: Roll out dough, place butter sheet in the center, fold, and roll. Repeat folding and rolling 3 times, chilling between folds.
  4. Shape: Roll dough into a rectangle, cut into triangles, and roll each into a crescent shape.
  5. Proof: Let shaped croissants rise until puffy.
  6. Bake: Brush with egg wash and bake at 375°F (190°C) until golden brown.
